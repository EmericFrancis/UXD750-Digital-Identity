What is the electoral register?

The electoral register is a list of people able to vote in a general election, and it details their names and addresses. It includes residents over the voting age who meet citizenship or residency criteria.

How we know this?

As an approved GOV.UK Verify identity provider, we can access the full electoral register under specific legal conditions to allow us to perform identity verification and credit checking. We are regularly audited to ensure the data is used in compliance with regulations.

Why we need to know this?

We need to access and ask these questions based on private data (typically financial history, electoral register or utility company data) that isn't publicly accessible, so people pretending to be you are unlikely to be able to answer the questions correctly. This is how we ensure that the person interacting with the service is who they claim to be to verify their identity digitally.
